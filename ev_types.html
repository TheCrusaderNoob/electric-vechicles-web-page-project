Introduction to Electric Vehicles

Electric vehicles (EVs) are changing the future of transportation. They are powered by electricity and have a wide range of environmental benefits.

Battery Electric Vehicles (BEVs)

BEVs are fully electric vehicles that run on electricity stored in batteries. They produce zero emissions and rely entirely on charging stations for energy.

Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles (PHEVs)

PHEVs combine a battery-powered electric motor with a traditional gasoline engine. They can operate on electricity for shorter distances and switch to fuel for longer trips.

Hybrid Electric Vehicles (HEVs)

HEVs use both an electric motor and an internal combustion engine, but they cannot be plugged in. The battery is charged through regenerative braking and the engine.

Fuel Cell Electric Vehicles (FCEVs)

FCEVs use hydrogen fuel cells to generate electricity. They emit only water vapor and have the potential for longer ranges compared to BEVs.
