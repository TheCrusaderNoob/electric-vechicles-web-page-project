Introduction to Electric Vehicles

Electric vehicles (EVs) are changing the future of transportation. They are powered by electricity and have a wide range of environmental benefits.

The Early Days (1800s)

Electric vehicles (EVs) date back to the early 19th century. In the 1830s, inventors in Hungary, the Netherlands, and the United States began creating small-scale electric vehicles.

The Golden Age (1900s)

By the early 1900s, electric cars were more popular than gasoline-powered vehicles due to their quietness and ease of use. However, mass production of gas-powered cars, like the Ford Model T, led to the decline of EVs.

Decline and Revival (1920s - 1990s)

As internal combustion engines improved and gasoline became widely available, electric cars nearly vanished. However, the 1970s oil crisis reignited interest in EVs, leading to limited production of electric models.

The Modern Era (2000s - Present)

The 21st century saw a surge in EV innovation, with companies like Tesla leading the charge. Government incentives and a growing focus on sustainability have made electric vehicles a key part of the future of transportation.
